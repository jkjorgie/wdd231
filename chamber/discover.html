Steel Days

Steel Days in American Fork, Utah, is a beloved annual tradition celebrating the city's heritage and community spirit. First established in 1945, the festival was inspired by the local steel industry, which significantly shaped the area's economic growth. The event has its roots in earlier celebrations like "Timber Day" and "Liberty Day," which date back to the late 19th century.

This week-long festival features a variety of activities, including a carnival, a parade, a car show, live performances, and fireworks. Other highlights include a garden tour, pickleball tournaments, and an art show, offering entertainment for all ages.

The American Fork Chamber of Commerce plays a key role in organizing Steel Days, with significant support from local businesses, ensuring that many activities are free to attend. For detailed schedules and updates about the 2025 Steel Days, visiting the official event website or the American Fork Chamber of Commerce is recommended.

Silicon Slopes

The city is a part of Utah's booming Silicon Slopes tech corridor, with numerous startups and tech companies calling the area home. This growth has helped American Fork maintain a balance between modern amenities and small-town charm.

Population & Growth

With a population of over 30,000, American Fork continues to grow while preserving its community-oriented atmosphere. Its downtown area showcases historic buildings and local businesses that add to its charm.
